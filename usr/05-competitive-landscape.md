# Competitive Landscape

> Internal analysis of who else occupies the AI transformation space

## Category Map

### Tier 1: Big Consultancies (Authority, Lead Gen)

| Player | Strengths | Weaknesses | Content Style |
|--------|-----------|------------|---------------|
| **Deloitte AI Institute** | Research (Pulse Check), RoA framing, credibility | Generic, sales-driven, gated reports | Whitepapers, blog series |
| **BCG** | Three value plays (Deploy/Reshape/Invent), executive focus | Expensive consulting implied, less practical | Featured insights, long-form |
| **McKinsey (QuantumBlack)** | Deep industry data, "Rewired" framework | Corporate tone, not accessible to mid-market | Reports, case studies |
| **KPMG** | Enterprise orchestration framing | PDF-heavy, less web-native | Large PDF reports |
| **CGI, Accenture, etc.** | Full lifecycle consulting narrative | Vendor/platform bias | Service marketing pages |

**Our angle:** Synthesize their research into practical, actionable content without the consulting sales funnel.

### Tier 2: Individual Strategists / Speakers

| Player | Strengths | Weaknesses |
|--------|-----------|------------|
| **Jordache Johnson** (jordache.ai) | Proprietary frameworks (ADAPT, 5 Invisible Forces), keynote focus | Personality-dependent, services-heavy |
| **Various LinkedIn creators** | High engagement, relatable | Shallow, trend-chasing, no depth |

**Our angle:** Framework-driven like strategists, but platform-independent and research-backed like consultancies.

### Tier 3: Vendor / Platform Blogs

| Player | Strengths | Weaknesses |
|--------|-----------|------------|
| **Databricks** | Excellent use case content | Platform-biased (lakehouse) |
| **AWS, Google Cloud, Microsoft** | Real customer stories (Covestro, BASF, Danone) | Cloud vendor lock-in narrative |
| **Atlan, Dataiku, etc.** | Deep governance/data content | Product marketing |

**Our angle:** Vendor-neutral pattern guidance (copilots, RAG, agents, automation) without platform preference.

### Tier 4: News & Aggregators

| Player | Strengths | Weaknesses |
|--------|-----------|------------|
| **The Batch (DeepLearning.AI)** | Curated AI news | Not transformation-focused |
| **Ben's Bites, TLDR AI** | Fast, comprehensive | No depth, no frameworks |
| **Generic "AI transformation" SEO sites** | Rank for keywords | Thin content, no POV |

**Our angle:** Depth over breadth. Not daily news — weekly transformation insights.

## What to Learn from Competitors

### From Deloitte
- Three gaps framework (work redesign, governance, ROI) — adopt and expand
- Pulse Check data format — consider annual "State of AI Transformation" report
- RoA concept — build content pillar around it

### From BCG
- Three value plays (Deploy, Reshape, Invent) — useful audience segmentation
- "Only 25% find value" stat — powerful hook for content

### From Jordache Johnson
- Proprietary framework branding (ADAPT™) — we should create our own
- "Monday morning actions" — every piece ends with concrete next steps
- Keynote/workshop as monetization — viable for later phase

### From Vendor Case Studies
- Specific metrics (99% cycle time reduction, 12hr → 6min) — use in use case content
- Pattern format: Problem → Approach → Architecture → Results → Lessons

## Competitive Risks

1. **Big consultancies publish more free research** — Hard to out-authority them; win on practicality
2. **AI hype cycle cools** — Transformation framing is more durable than tool hype
3. **New domain competitors** — `.io` exact match is a moat; move fast on SEO
4. **LLM-generated SEO spam** — Quality and original frameworks are the defense

## Monitoring Plan

Track quarterly:
- New reports from Deloitte, BCG, McKinsey, KPMG, Economist
- Google search results for target keywords (who ranks?)
- New entrants in "AI transformation" space
- LinkedIn engagement on transformation topics (what resonates?)

## Strategic Recommendation

Don't compete on volume. Compete on:
1. **Original frameworks** (Three Gaps Diagnostic, Autonomy Ladder)
2. **Practical synthesis** of research others publish but don't actionablize
3. **Decision-centric perspective** others miss
4. **Mid-market accessibility** big consultancies ignore

The domain `ai-transformation.io` gives a head start on SEO. The content strategy in `knowledge-base/` provides the substance to keep it.
